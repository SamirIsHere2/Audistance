AU DISTANCE — CAMERA-GUIDED HEARING RANGE

Find your hearing sweet spot.

Center your face in the frame. AU Distance estimates how far you are from the camera and flags whether you're sitting in a generally recommended range for clear face-to-face hearing and lip visibility — useful for conversations, video calls, or hearing device fitting checks.

01 What this does

AU Distance watches your webcam feed locally in this browser tab, finds your face, and estimates how far you are from the screen. It flags whether you're inside a recommended range (default 90–150 cm / ≈3–5 ft) for comfortable face-to-face hearing and lip visibility.

Nothing is recorded or uploaded. Video frames are processed on your device and are never sent anywhere.

02 Turn on the camera

Go to the Monitor tab and click Turn on camera. Your browser will ask you to grant camera permission — that prompt has to come from your browser directly, so click "Allow" when it appears. Nothing turns your camera on automatically or without that prompt.

Once it's on, center your face in frame. The four indicators (distance, horizontal centering, vertical centering, lighting) turn green when you're in a good spot.

03 Distance-based volume

Turn on Volume by distance in the Monitor tab and this happens automatically as you move:

  • Move closer to the camera → volume turns down.
  • Move farther away → volume turns up.

This can control two things independently: a built-in test tone played on this page (no setup needed), and/or your Spotify playback volume (needs a one-time connection, see below). Either way, this tool can only ever change volume it plays itself or that it explicitly controls through Spotify's API — it can never touch your device's system volume or any other app.

04 Connect Spotify (optional)

To let distance control your actual Spotify playback volume, you'll need your own free Spotify developer app — this keeps your login credentials entirely between you and Spotify, this page never sees your password.

  • Go to developer.spotify.com/dashboard and log in with your Spotify account.
  • Click Create app. Give it any name and description.
  • In Redirect URIs, add the exact URL this page is hosted at (shown on the Monitor tab once you're there).
  • Save, then copy the app's Client ID into the Monitor tab and click Connect Spotify.

You'll be sent to Spotify to log in and approve access, then returned here. You'll also need Spotify Premium and a song already playing on an active device — Spotify's API can only adjust volume on a session that's currently playing, it can't start playback for you.

05 Good to know

The distance number is an approximation — it assumes an average adult face width and a typical webcam field of view, so treat it as a general guide, not a clinical measurement.

Because this page doesn't use browser storage, your Spotify connection and Client ID reset if you reload the page or navigate away — you'll reconnect with one click, but it won't stay signed in long-term.

Let AU Distance adjust in-page volume?
Turning this on lets AU Distance raise or lower the volume of audio played on this page (like the test tone above) based on how far you are from the camera — quieter as you move closer, louder as you move farther away.

This can't touch your computer's system volume or any other app or tab — only sound played here.
Let AU Distance adjust Spotify volume?
Turning this on lets AU Distance raise or lower the playback volume on your active Spotify device based on how far you are from the camera — quieter as you move closer, louder as you move farther away, using Spotify's official volume control API.

It can only change volume on the device that's currently playing — it can't start, stop, skip, or change what's playing, and it can't touch your system volume.
Distance is an approximation. It assumes an average adult face width (~14 cm) and a typical webcam field of view, so treat the number as a general guide rather than a clinical measurement.
